MISSIONXPLORER

A 3D multitouch sketch board for designing military operations

Role(s)

Product Lead, Software Developer, UX/UI Designer

Team

3-5 Software Engineers, 1-2 Systems Engineers

Technology

vizrt Python MongoDB

Description

MissionXplorer is a modeling and simulation tool which lets a user design and simulate military missions to perform trade studies on sensor, weapon and platform combinations against various threats. It would be analogous to an app that lets a football coach simulate a play from his playbook against various teams and formations so that he/she can better prepare for future games.

Users can publish the mission to one or many simulation engines which run the mission to varying degrees of fidelity. Key metrics are collected over a set of monte carlo trials and displayed back to the user for analysis. Example end users include: Naval officers, operations analysts, engineers

MissionXplorer is an on-going 2 year rapid prototyping project being developed at Lockheed Martin.

AlertMe

A Google Glass heads-up display for combat ship operators.

Role(s)

Product Lead, Software Developer, UX/UI Designer

Team

1 Software Developer (myself)

Technology

Google Glass Android Python MySQL

Description

AlertMe is an application that runs on Google Glass, which is worn by naval officers on-board an active combat ship. The purpose of the application is to alert the user of important system events happening on the ship during its operation.

Events include warnings/errors such as equipment failures, informational alerts such as equipment status changes, or critical events such as weapon engagements. Once an alert is viewed by the user, he/she can dismiss it or delete it from the system through voice commands or by using Google Glass's touch controls.

AlertMe was a short experimental project developed at Lockheed Martin in the Surface Navy Innovation Center.

JointSafe

A wireless wearable sensor system for monitoring hip joint motion during rehabilitation after surgery

Role(s)

Software Developer, Electrical Engineer

Team

2 Engineers (myself included)

Technology

Android Arduino Atmel Microcontrollers Motorola Xoom Tablet

Description

This project explores the use of wearable sensors to continuously monitor joint motion in patients that are recovering from some sort of joint replacement surgery. The JointSafe system is a prototype which focuses specifically on the hip joint.

To give some context, when a patient undergoes hip replacement surgery, the motion of their hip joint is severely restricted during recovery. If the patient violates any of the restrictions, they are in danger of dislocating their hip and then they must go back for another hip replacement surgery. For example, hip rehab patients cannot bend down to the point where the angle between their chest and thigh is less than 90 degrees. If they do so, they risk dislocating the hip joint.

JointSafe is a wireless 3 sensor system that monitors the motion of the hip joint, reports real-time motion data to an Android tablet, and also provides the patient with early warning through vibration when they are nearing violation of any motion restrictions.
